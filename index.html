Overview
• Target 16' logs for 60% or more of the load
• Limit 10' logs to no more than 10 per load
• Minimize the number of 10' and 14' logs on loads
• The mills do not accept 8' logs
• Keep pallet grade logs to a minimum
• Trim excessive butt flares; trim limbs flush
• Logs without enough trim will be scaled back to next nominal length
Minimum Specifications
Trim: Logs with insufficient trim scaled back to next nominal length. 6" trim. Maximum length 16'10"; minimum 60% 16' logs by volume per load; minimum length 10'6"; 10 per load maximum. NO 8' logs accepted.

7–10" dia. logs: No rot, sweep, crook, or insect damage
Stain/wormholes: Fresh, live wood delivered within seven days of cutting (April–Sept.); no blue stain or wormholes allowed
Sweep: Up to 1-1/2" per 8' accepted without deduction
Seams: One straight seam acceptable
Deductions: No pencil rot; up to 50% of diameter, well-centered rot allowed with deduction in logs 11"+ only; scale deductions taken for knots over 5"
Preparation: Limbs trimmed flush; no double tops
Metal: Logs with iron or iron stain will not be accepted
Cull logs: Become mill property 10 days after delivery

Scaling rule difference
Hancock uses the International 1/4" rule; Irving uses the International 1/8" rule. The 1/8" rule yields higher scale — the same physical log will produce more board feet at Irving. This affects both the check received and how deductions are calculated. When comparing prices per MBF between mills, account for this difference.
Grade name mapping — Hancock vs. Irving
Hancock Select ≈ Irving Select (both 14" min.; similar knot tolerance on clear faces)
Hancock #1 Long ≈ Irving 16 ft. Sawlog (16' logs; Hancock 7" min. vs. Irving 5" min.)
Hancock #1 Short ≈ Irving Short Sawlog (10'–14'; knot tolerance similar)
Hancock #2 — no direct Irving equivalent; Irving goes from Sawlog to Pallet
Hancock #3 Pallet ≈ Irving Long Pallet / Pallet

Irving accepts 5" top diameter on sawlogs; Hancock requires 7"+ for #1. Small-top logs that make sawlog at Irving may be #2 or pallet at Hancock.
Stain policy
Irving: None acceptable (year-round)
Hancock: No blue stain or wormholes; live wood within 7 days of cutting (April–Sept.)
Irving's stain policy is stricter and year-round. Stained material should be separated before delivery to Irving.
